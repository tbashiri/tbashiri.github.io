## Overview

My research focuses on high-resolution laser spectroscopy of calcium-containing molecular systems, with applications to precision measurement, molecular structure determination, and isotope-resolved spectroscopy.

I am broadly interested in experimental AMO physics and chemical physics, particularly in developing and applying spectroscopic techniques to extract molecular constants and isotope-sensitive observables.

---

## Calcium Isotope Spectroscopy

This work focuses on precision spectroscopic measurements of calcium isotopologues, with the goal of extracting molecular constants and studying isotope-dependent effects in molecular structure.

Key aspects:
- Extraction of molecular constants from rovibronic spectra  
- Comparison of isotopologues (e.g., \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(^{42}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(^{44}\mathrm{Ca}\))  
- Analysis of isotope shifts and hyperfine structure  
- Contribution to understanding isotope-dependent molecular physics  

---

## CaF Molecular Spectroscopy

We study the \(A^2\Pi - X^2\Sigma^+\) band system of CaF, focusing on multiple isotopologues.

This work includes:
- Spectroscopic fits of rovibronic transitions  
- Determination of fine and hyperfine structure parameters  
- Comparison across isotopologues  
- High-resolution spectral modeling  

This system provides a benchmark for precision molecular spectroscopy and tests of molecular structure theory.

---

## Spatial Heterodyne Spectroscopy

We develop and apply spatial heterodyne spectroscopy (SHS) techniques for high-resolution molecular spectroscopy.

Key goals include:
- Enhancing spectral resolution without moving parts  
- Improving signal-to-noise in broadband molecular spectra  
- Enabling compact, high-stability spectroscopic measurements  
- Application to molecular beam and gas-phase spectroscopy  

---

## Experimental Approach

My work combines:
- Laser-based spectroscopy techniques  
- High-resolution spectral acquisition  
- Least-squares fitting of molecular spectra  
- Computational analysis of rovibronic structure  

The goal is to connect measured spectra to physically meaningful molecular constants with quantified uncertainties.
